Mahatma Gandhi

The only Indian who employed non violence resistance to lead successful campaign for India's independence against British.

Mahatma Gandhi, whose leadership brought freedom, was in Kolkata, spinning and fasting as India celebrated its first Independence Day on August 15, 1947.

Here's a timeline of Mahatma Gandhi's life:

  • 1869 - Born in Porbandar, Gujarat, India
  • 1882 - Married with Kasturibai, aged 13.
  • 1894 - Founded the 'Natal Indian Congress'
  • 1899 - Established Indian Ambulance Corp during the boer war.
  • 1906 - Advent of Sathyagraha at Empire Theatre, Johannesburg.
  • 1915 - Established 'Satyagraha Ashram', Kochrab
  • 1919 - Satyagraha pledge against 'Rowlett Act
  • 1924 - Presided over the Belgaum Congress.
  • 1925 - All India Spinners' Association' established.
  • 1928 - Boycott of Simon Commission.
  • 1929 - Viceroy Lord Irwin annouces Round Table Conference.
  • 1930 - Dandi March commences from Satyagraha Ashram and broke the salt law.
  • 1931 - Gandhi-Irwin pact signed.
  • 1932 - Congress Working Committee adopted the resolution of Civil Disobedience and signed the Poona Pact.
  • 1933 - Nationwide Harijan Yatra commenced.
  • 1942 - Quit India' resolution adopted; gave the call 'Do or Die'
  • 1944 - Death of Kasturba; cremated at Aga Khan Palace.
  • 1946 - A 12 member Interim Government headed by Pandit Nehru formed.
  • 1947 - The Congress, the Muslim League and the representatives of the Sikhs accept the plan of Partition.
  • 1948 - Took three bullets on his chest. Godse the assassin and Cremated on the banks of the Yamuna by Ramdas.
